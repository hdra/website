There's a particular kind of stillness that exists in cities — not absence of noise, but pockets where the noise stops mattering for a moment.

I spent a morning walking without a destination, which is the only way I know how to find these places. A side street still wet from rain. A warung with two empty stools. A man reading a newspaper in front of a shuttered shop.

The light in Jakarta before eight is softer than you'd expect. It comes in sideways and catches the texture of everything — walls, faces, the steam rising from a cart.

I didn't talk to anyone. I just walked and looked, which felt like enough.
